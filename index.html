Varmints is a tiny ecosystem simulator. The rabbits eat the grass, the foxes eat the rabbits. Adjust the attributes of the grass, rabbits, and foxes to see how it affects the development of your ecosystem, or leave them on their defaults to see what happens. But with each generation, the animals mutate, and through survival of the fittest, each species evolves. Try to keep the system in balance as long as possible! To see what each attribute does, click on the question mark next to it. When you're satisfied with the ecosystem's statistics, click the 'Begin Eco-simulation' button and LOOK AT EM GO!

Grass

Nutrition is the amount of energy a rabbit gains when eating a single unit of grass

Grass needs other grasses near it to create new grass. If you increase the spawn distance, the grass pollen can travel farther away, allowing the grass to mate with other grasses farther away.

×

Spawn threshold is the number of nearby grasses that a grass needs to reproduce.

×

Reach is how far away a grass can send its seed and grow a new grass.

Rabbits

Starting population is the number of rabbits placed in the ecosystem at the beginning of the simulation.

×

Maximum age is the age at which a rabbit dies.

×

Starting energy is the amount of energy that the first rabbits are created with.

×

Speed is how quickly the rabbit moves through the environment. Faster speeds use up more energy.

×

Awareness is how far away a rabbit can see grass, mates, and predators. Higher awareness uses more energy.

×

Greed is the maximum energy level at which rabbits will seek out food. Above this level, they will not eat.

×

Libido is the minimum energy level at which a rabbit will mate. Rabbits give a portion of their energy to their children, so they might not want to mate when they are hungry.. A higher number means the rabbit will be more cautious about mating.

×

Gestation duration is the amount of time, in seconds, a rabbit stays pregnant before giving birth.

×

Litter size is the number of babies the rabbit will have during each pregnancy.

×

Parental investment is the amount of energy the rabbit will give to their children, from .01(1%) to 1 (100%).

×

Sense efficiency is the amount of energy used by the rabbit's senses. A higher number means less energy is used.

×

Speed efficiency is the amouunt of energy used by the rabbit's movement. A higher number means less energy is used.
